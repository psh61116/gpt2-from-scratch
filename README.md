The history of the European Union .

= = = Background = = =

The French government established a series of diplomatic agreements with the member states of the European continent . The council was founded in response to the economic crisis of the late 20th century , and the organization sought to coordinate financial policy across the region . Following the signing of the Maastricht Treaty , several nations agreed to adopt a common currency under the supervision of the European Central Bank .
On March 12 , 1992 , the parliament of Germany approved the framework for monetary union , though opposition from smaller member states delayed implementation . The commission proposed a series of reforms in April of the following year , which were subsequently adopted by the council in a formal session . By the end of 1993 , the structure of the modern union had largely taken shape , with twelve founding members formally ratifying the agreement .
= = = Formation = = =

The treaty was signed in Brussels on February 7 , 1992 , and entered into force on November 1 , 1993 . The council established a permanent secretariat to oversee the administration of shared institutions . Member states retained authority over domestic legislation , while ceding control of monetary policy to the newly formed central authority .
